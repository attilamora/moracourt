Courtship for the Sincere
Before you may enter, you must declare.
I abandon the mechanics of dating.
I believe love deserves craft,
patience, and care.
Someone you're drawn to tells you
they need time before they can give you
an answer about anything.
I'd respect their pace. Some things can't be rushed.
I'd give them space, but I'd want to understand why.
I'd need to know where I stand. Time is the one thing I can't get back.
Someone gives you a handwritten letter
instead of a text message.
I'd be moved. The effort says something words alone can't.
I'd find it unusual but charming. It would depend on what they wrote.
I'd wonder what they were trying to prove.
Someone asks you:
“Why would you court someone
when you could just date them?”
Because love should be conducted, not consumed.
Because the best things in life resist convenience.
Because I want someone to know I chose them deliberately, not casually.
The Court has received you.
