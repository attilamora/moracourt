The Lineage
“In Érd, in a windowless 25-square-metre workshop,
the great-grandfather kept tailoring.
Not because anyone was watching.
Because a Mora cannot stop
making beautiful things.”
The Court of MÓRA was not founded in 2026. It existed for generations. When the communists came, they seized the assets, destroyed the homes, built panel housing where gardens once grew. The Court didn't die. It went underground.
The grandmother sold lampshades door to door. The great-grandfather tailored suits by night for politicians and elites. The mother escaped in 1980, worked three jobs, 14–16 hour days, rose from employee to business owner within two years. The craft continued in secret.
Three generations later, the concrete cracks.
The garden breaks through.

The Court is not launching.
The Court is reopening.
